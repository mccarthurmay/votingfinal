Deegan-Packel Brief History

J. Deegan Jr.

University of Rochester

Edward Packel

Lake Forest College

J. Deegan Jr. and Edward Packel wanted to create a system that can be more broadly used than Banzhaf and Shapley-Shubik’s systems. The Deegan-Packel solution is able to generalize the power index and axioms provided to a broader group of situations. The new generalized version of a power index can now be extended to more complicated situations with different rules and characteristics applied, which Banzhaf and Shapley-Shubik may not be able to reach. The Deegan Packel index chooses to depend on the number of minimal winning coalitions*. Every voter within the minimal winning coalition gains a point. Each individual voter’s points are then divided by the total number of points in the system to get an individual’s Deegan-Packel power index.
*minimal winning coalition = a group of individuals that reached the quota, therefore, they won the vote. However, no a single individual can leave or else the group will lose the vote.
